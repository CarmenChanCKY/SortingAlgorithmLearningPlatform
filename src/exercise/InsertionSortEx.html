Q1. How many times of looping is needed to sort number 4 3 8 2 1 in ascending order by insertion sort?
A. 4 times
B. 5 times
C. 6 times
D. 7 times

Explanation:
element= 4|3 8 2 1
After 1st loop: 3 4|8 2 1
After 2nd loop: 3 4 8|2 1
After 3rd loop: 2 3 4 8|1
After 4th loop:: 1 2 3 4 8
The number has been sorted.

Q2. How many times of looping is needed to sort number 6 1 2 9 4 7 in descending order by insertion sort?
A. 3 times
B. 4 times
C. 5 times
D. 6 times

Explanation:
element= 6|1 2 9 4 7
After 1st loop: 1 6|2 9 4 7
After 2nd loop: 1 2 6|9 4 7
After 3rd loop: 1 2 6 9|4 7
After 4th loop: 1 2 4 6 9|7
After 5th loop: 1 2 4 6 7 9
The number has been sorted.

Q3. Which answer(s) about Insertion Sort is correct?
A. Comparison will start at the second element
B. All the number can be sorted within 5 times of looping
C. The starting position is i-1th element when sort in descending order
D. Nested-loop is needed for insertion sort

Explanation:
For answer B, number of loops is depends on element size.
For example, consider element 6|1 2 9 4 7 3
After 1st loop: 1 6|2 9 4 7 3
After 2nd loop: 1 2 6|9 4 7 3
After 3rd loop: 1 2 6 9|4 7 3
After 4th loop: 1 2 4 6 9|7 3
After 5th loop: 1 2 4 6 7 9|3
After 6th loop: 1 2 3 4 6 7 9
In this case, 6 looping is need to sort this element. So answer B is incorrect.

Q3. Insertion sort is an example of an incremental algorithm.
True
False

Reference: https://www.sanfoundry.com/insertion-sort-interview-questions-answers/

Explanation:
Explanation: In the incremental algorithms, the complicated structure on n items is built by first building it on n - 1 items. And then we make the necessary changes to fix things in adding the last item. Insertion sort builds the sorted sequence one element at a time. Therefore, it is an example of an incremental algorithm.

Q3. Consider the code given below, which runs insertion sort:

                            void insertionSort(int arr[], int array_size)
                            {
                              int i, j, value;
                              for (i = 1; i < array_size; i++)
                              {
                                      value = arr[i];
                                      j = i;
                                      while (________ )
                                      {
                                               arr[j] = arr[j - 1];
                                               j = j - 1;
                                      }
                                      arr[j] = value;
                              }
                            }

Which condition will correctly implement the while loop?
A. (j > 0) && (arr[j + 1] > value)
B. (j > 0) && (arr[j - 1] > value)
C. (j > 0) && (arr[j + 1] < value)
D. (j > 0) || (arr[j - 1] > value)

Reference: https://www.sanfoundry.com/insertion-sort-interview-questions-answers/

Explanation:
In insertion sort, the element is A[j] is inserted into the correct position in the sorted sequence A[1...j - 1]. So, condition given in (j > 0) && (arr[j - 1] > value) will implement while loop correctly.
