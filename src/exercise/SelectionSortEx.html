Q1. Which one is the answer of sorting array A={6, 0, 2, 9, 4} in ascending order after 2nd looping?
A. 0 2 4 9 6
B. 0 2 6 9 4
C. 0 6 9 4 2
D. 0 6 2 9 4

Explanation:
element= 6|0 2 9 4
After 1st loop: 0 6|2 9 4
After 2nd loop: 0 2 6|9 4
After 3rd loop: 0 2 4 9|6
After 4th loop: 0 2 4 6 9
The whole element is sorted.

Q2. How many times of looping is needed to sort number 8 0 7 1 9 3 4 in descending order by selection sort?
A. 3 times
B. 4 times
C. 5 times
D. 6 times

Explanation:
element= 8|0 7 1 9 3 4
After 1st loop: 0 8|7 1 9 3 4
After 2nd loop: 0 1 7|8 9 3 4
After 3rd loop: 0 1 3 8|9 7 4
After 4th loop: 0 1 3 4 9|7 8
After 5 th loop: 0 1 3 4 7 9|8
After 6th loop: 0 1 3 4 7 8 9
The whole element is sorted. Early termination condition will work after 6th loop.

Q3. Which answer(s) about selection Sort is correct?
A. The performance of selection sort depends on the order of list element.
B. Early-termination will occur when there are no swapping in the whole list
C. Comparison will start at the second element
D. The number of loops is needed to sort a list is depends on the list size

For answer A, the worst case, average case and best case time complexity of selection sort are Ω(n2), Θ(n2) and Ο(n2). It shows the performance of selection sort is not related to the order of list element. So, A is incorrect.

For answer D, number of loops is needed to sort a list is related to element size.
Each list needs at most n-1 times of looping to sort the list. For example, to sort 1 2 6 4 5 3 in ascending order needs 3 loops.
Sorting 1 6 3 9 8 in descending order needs 4 loops.
So, D is correct.

Q4. How many times of looping is needed to sort number 4 2 1 3 6 in ascending order by selection sort?

B. 3 times
C. 4 times
D. 5 times

Explanation:

After 3rd loop: 1 2 3 4|6
The whole element is sorted. Early termination condition will work after 3rd loop.
